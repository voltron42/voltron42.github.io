Shadows Over Dale

This is Faerun: a world of incredible beauty, sprawling wonder, mystical heritage, and in some places... ages old evil.

The free peoples of Faerun push back at the unbridled wilds with cities and culture, while the unknown shadows of fallen ruins and endless caverns fill the belly of these lands with a promise of danger and glory.

The gods take active interest in the shaping of the world through their worshippers, while the pursuit of arcane potential ever brings closer an age of renewed prosperity or destruction.

This is the world in which you live and our adventure begins with the place you all currently call home; nestled between the Thunder Peaks and the forests and rivers of The Dalelands lies the previously sleepy town of Highmoon, protected and ruled by the Baron Crispian Dale.

Sentinel of Peril

You have been recruited to rescue the Baron's children, who have been abducted by a raid by goblins. You tracked the goblin raiders to their hideout; the ancestral tomb of the noble family. Inside the tomb you discovered a secret entrance to a lower level hidden under the statue of a soldier. You followed the spiral staircase downward, where you fought a gargoyle, avoided a potentially trapped treasure chest and some manner of screeching, skittering creatures which you chose not to face, and instead ran away.

A Test Of Honor

No prisoner, no clues, and only questions, Corellien resolves to solve the riddle of the throne room. After much discussion, and a few attempts, Garin finds that, as he kneels before the throne and swears his fealty to the family of Dale, the dias and the throne upon it slides to the side, revealing a door. Ellery, Trogdof and Garin take the lead down the hallway, where Ellery finds a series of floor tiles seem to be pressure-triggers for a poison gas trap. You all make your way carefully down the hallway, avoiding the trigger tiles, and towards the door at the other end.

As the party enters the room, a stone slab drops into place, completely blocking the door. Arcane crystal wall sconces begin to light, revealing a large room containing 7 sarcophagi, the last being guarded by the statue of a large bird of prey (a falcon or eagle). The statue begins to glow blue, and then the glow separates itself from the statue, then reformes into a 7' tall humanoid figure, still translucent, with feathered wings, blue skin, long silver hair, simple robes, golden weapons and armor, and a blank expression. One by one, he challenges your honor, daring you to prove yourselves. As he does this, each of the other six sarcophagi are opened from the inside by the newly animated armored skeletons, each of whom wields a different special weapon or item. The battle with the skeletons is challenging, but you are ultimately triumphant, as the skeletons crumble beneath your fists, blades, and Trogdof's "Worldstar!"

You turn back toward the blue man, and see that, while he remains translucent, his form begins to shift again. His form shrinks by a head, his skin turns pinkish, his robes turn blue and grow longer, his armor turns gray, and the rest of his clothes shift and change around him. He smiles amiably at each of you, and speaks nervously. He introduces himself as the astral form of Kael Vardon, one of the legendary Seven, the heroes of the Land of Dale who drove out the Zhentarim two centuries past. As he speaks, he turns to the sarcophagus behind his form and waves his hand. The lid splits open in the middle, each end slides as they open, and an old, old cittern levitates up out of the sarcophagus and into Kael's ethereal hands. He explains that this is one of the seven Instruments of the Bards, which were what the Seven had used in their ultimate victory over the Zhentarim. "This is the MacFuirmidh Cittern," he says, " and it is now entrusted to you." He also explains that the other Instruments of the Bards have been hidden throughout the Land of Dale, and will be needed for the challenge that you yet face. Realizing that he has just given away more information than he should have, he quickly bids you farewell and good luck, and vanishes with a twinkle. As he does, the stone barrier blocking the door retracts, and the door opens.
