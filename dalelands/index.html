Shadows Over Dale

This is Faerun: a world of incredible beauty, sprawling wonder, mystical heritage, and in some places... ages old evil.

The free peoples of Faerun push back at the unbridled wilds with cities and culture, while the unknown shadows of fallen ruins and endless caverns fill the belly of these lands with a promise of danger and glory.

The gods take active interest in the shaping of the world through their worshippers, while the pursuit of arcane potential ever brings closer an age of renewed prosperity or destruction.

This is the world in which you live and our adventure begins with the place you all currently call home; nestled between the Thunder Peaks and the forests and rivers of The Dalelands lies the previously sleepy town of Highmoon, protected and ruled by the Baron Crispian Dale.

Sentinel of Peril

You have been recruited to rescue the Baron's children, who have been abducted by a raid by goblins. You tracked the goblin raiders to their hideout; the ancestral tomb of the noble family. Inside the tomb you discovered a secret entrance to a lower level hidden under the statue of a soldier. You followed the spiral staircase downward, where you fought a gargoyle, avoided a potentially trapped treasure chest and some manner of screeching, skittering creatures which you chose not to face, and instead ran away.

Blessings and Bad Luck

Returning to the main hall of the tomb, you follow the goblin tracks to confront them in a room containing an enchanted fountain, chasing the last survivor further into the tomb where Trogdof fell into a pit trap. After helping Trogdof out of the trap, you attempted to sneak into the room where the children were being kept, not realizing that your foes had already become aware of your presence. After Willow was wounded to unconsciousness in the following battle, Garin's rage, Cor's insults, and Trogof's critical execution of a Magic Missle made short work of your foes and allowed you to free the children and leave a single hobgoblin caged in the tomb.

You return to the city of Highmoon, the Baron's children resting in Cor's wagon with the heavily wounded but stable ranger.

For Love Of Money

Your expedient and successful return to Highmoon is greeted by cheering townsfolk, an awed Captain Damon Velhok of the Baron's Guard, and a grateful Baron and Baronness. The cheers and applause of the townspeople follow you through the town to the Manor, where the Baron and Baronness greet you graciously but briefly before ushering the children back in to the Manor, after which the Baron's (colorful) secretary grants you your reward, relays the Baron's order / invitation to a debriefing the next morning, and introduces you to a cleric, Fezzik, who has some sort of awkward past with Garin. Your business concluded and at your requests for a hot bath, a good meal, and a good night's sleep, Fezzik leads you to the Oak & Spear Tavern, where you are greeted as heroes, and gain your requests.

Following A Darker Path

As you leave Hanseld's Emporium, now with two new party members, your newly befriended cleric stumbles, freezes, and convulses momentarily. When he recovers, he takes off running down a side street. Fed up and looking for a confrontation with this cowardly exile, Garin tells the others to go to the debriefing without them, and takes off running after Fezzik.

The rest of the party, mildly confused, decides to head to the Manor, taking your time to take in some of the sights of the city now that you can see it in the daylight. You stroll throught the business laden main street of the city, then wanders up the more affluent district of town. After proper introductions by the Baron with his wife the Baroness Azani, his sister the Countess Carolinia, sovreign of Arabel, the Countess' spouse Dr. Zana Haranthi, and a Professor Charles Harcourt of the House Of Knowledge And Invention (who then left abruptly before the meeting), the Baron explained to all of you (sans dwarves) that, while the goblins had made their way out of the manor and city by very direct and public routes, killing a number of guards in the process, they had somehow managed to distract the guards in such a way that left the main western gate completely abandoned, allowing them to escape. Moreover, while their exit had been public, there was no trace of how they had managed to get into the city, the manor, or the childrens' room in the first place.

Hot On The Trail

After stepping out of Hanseld's Emporium, Fezzik's vision goes white. He sees the reflection of Captain Damon as the Captain looks in a mirror in a common area of the Watchmen's barracks. When the Captain turns away from the mirror to look back at Fezzik, it is no longer the Captain, but one of the other watchmen; Lt. Kavrik Gaines. The Leutenant turns back to the mirror, and the face is once again that of the Captain's, only slightly distorted, with the eyes turning a sickly yellow and beginning to glow. As the glow engulfs Fezzik's vision and turns white, Fezzik's vision clears, he is overtaken by a sense of urgency, and he takes off running to his temple, seeking clues to the vision's meaning, with Garin following hard upon.

The dwarves arrive at the Temple Of Moradin to meet with Keeper Arnak Kovak, who advises them that the vision indicates some sort of either danger or subterfuge around the Captain and the Leutenant. Not knowing the location of the Leutenant, but knowing that the Captain is supposed to be at the debriefing, the dwarves decide to head to the Manor. Arriving there, they find the meeting disolved, the different parties all attending to different issues having to do with the discovery of the secret passageways, and no sign of the Captain. The dwarves decide to journey into the sewers and attempt to catch up to the rest of the party. Eventually seeing the glowing Trogdof in the distance, and after yet another awkward attempt to cross the T-junction, the dwarves are reunited with the party.

Now reunited, the six of you continue to follow the goblin tracks through the sewer. after meandering through a couple more twists and turns, you discover a small outcropping where you find a dead body dressed in the clothing of the watchmen. Upon further inspection, you find that it is the body of Captain Damon, along with a pair of goblin tracks leading from the body to the rest of the tracks, and two pairs of human tracks leading to the body from another direction. After some debate, your party decides to continue to follow the goblin tracks with the expectation of finding an exit.
